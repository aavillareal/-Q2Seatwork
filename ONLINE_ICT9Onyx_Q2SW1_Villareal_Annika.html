Dog Breeds

Image Breed Description
Golden Retriever The medium-sized, well-built Golden Retriever is known for its glossy, glistening golden coat, which gives the breed its name. The large head, with its pleasant and intelligent eyes, short ears, and straight muzzle, is a breed trademark.
Poodle Poodles are incredibly smart and simple to train. They enjoy and succeed in a range of canine activities, including agility, obedience, and tracking. They are graceful, agile, and intelligent. They compete in dock diving and retriever hunt tests and are exceptional water retrievers.
Beagle Beagles have a slightly domed cranium and are strong, muscular dogs. Their snout is large, and their muzzle is square. Long and drooping ears. They have a deep chest, a straight back, and a somewhat long tail carried high.
Shih Tzu The Shih Tzu is a little dog that is strong and has a thick, long double coat. This breed is favored by fans of toy dogs because of its alert, self-assured, lively, and brave temperament. Shih Tzus are an ancient breed that have a long history of serving as nobles' lap dogs.
Greyhound The Greyhound is streamlined, robust, and capable of traveling at a top speed of around 45 miles per hour (72 km/h). It has a long neck, a deep chest, long, strong hindquarters, and a long, thin tail. It also has a narrow head.
French Bulldog With the exception of the enormous, upright "bat ears," which are the breed's defining characteristic, the French Bulldog looks like a small Bulldog. The nose is incredibly short, and the skull is huge and square with deep wrinkles wrapped over it. The body is compact and muscular underneath the glossy, dazzling coat.
Shiba The Shiba Inu dog breed was developed in Japan about 2,300 years ago for hunting small animals and ground birds. It weighs 17 to 23 pounds and is a strong dog that stands 13.5 to 16.5 inches (34 to 42 cm) tall at the shoulders (7.5 to 10.5 kg). Shiba Inus are renowned for their self-assurance, perkiness, and triangularly set eyes.
Chow-Chow Chows are large, powerful canines that can reach a shoulder height of 20 inches. A lion's mane ruff around the head and shoulders, a blue-black tongue, deep-set almond eyes that add to a scowling, haughty attitude, and a stiff-legged walk are some of their distinguishing features.
Pug The Pug was bred to be a friend and companion for his owners. He is charming, has a level and steady temperament, and is outgoing and loving. Pugs are typically simple to train since they strive to please their owners. Since they are prone to emotional fragility, severe training techniques should never be applied.
Corgi Among the most agreeable of all small housedogs, the Pembroke Welsh Corgi is a strong, athletic, and lively little herder who is affectionate and companionable without being needy. They are one the world's most popular herding breeds.
